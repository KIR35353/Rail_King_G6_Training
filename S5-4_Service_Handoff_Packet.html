Service Handoff Packet
Section 5
What the Service Handoff Packet Contains
When a machine is handed to service — planned or unplanned — the operator assembles this packet. It is the service team's starting point and must be complete before the machine is accepted.
📋
Completed Shift Log (all entries current)
Every shift entry from the last service event, including fault codes, fluid top-ups, and anomaly notes. Not just the most recent shift.
MANDATORY
⚠
Defect Report Form
Completed equipment tech support form (form_equip_tech_support) describing the fault: what happened, when, under what conditions, and how often it occurred.
MANDATORY
🔢
Current Hour Meter Reading
Recorded at the moment of service handoff — not from memory or the last log entry. The service team uses this to determine which scheduled maintenance items are due.
MANDATORY
📸
Photos of Visible Defects
If the defect involves visible damage, leaks, or wear — photograph before handing off. Photos are attached to the defect report or sent with the tech support form.
IF APPLICABLE
🔑
Keys and Access Items
Machine key, fuel card (if applicable), and any access codes or site-specific items the service technician will need to perform the work.
IF APPLICABLE
Handoff Flow — Operator to Service
The packet moves through three hands. Each stage has a defined responsibility.
👷
Operator
Completes shift log, defect report, and hour meter record. Assembles packet. Secures machine. Notifies supervisor of out-of-service status.
▼
📎
Supervisor / Site Coordinator
Reviews packet for completeness. Confirms machine is locked out. Submits tech support form to dealer or service contact. Sets repair priority.
▼
🔧
Service Technician
Receives complete packet. Uses shift log history and defect report to diagnose before opening the machine. Returns machine with a signed service record.
Incomplete packets create delays. A service technician who arrives without a defect report, shift log, or hour meter reading must spend diagnostic time reconstructing what the operator already knew. Every missing piece adds time and cost to the repair.
The operator's job ends when the packet is complete — not when the key is handed over. The packet is as important as securing the machine.

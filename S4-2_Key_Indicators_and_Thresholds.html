Key Indicators and Thresholds
Section 4
Engine Coolant
82–97°C
Normal Operating Band
Below 82 °C — warm-up not complete. Above 97 °C — reduce load and monitor. Above 105 °C — shut down.
Engine Oil Pressure
35–65psi
Operating Pressure Range
Pressure below 35 psi at operating speed is a red-lamp condition. Shut down and investigate immediately.
Hydraulic Oil Temp
50–80°C
Normal Operating Band
Below 50 °C — do not load circuits. Above 80 °C — reduce duty cycle. Above 90 °C — cease hydraulic actuation.
Air Reservoir
100–125psi
Operating Pressure Band
Below 85 psi — compressor rebuild phase. Do not release park brake until primary reaches minimum operating pressure.

Reading Trend vs. Snapshot
A single reading within range is not sufficient. Monitor for trend — a value that is climbing steadily toward the upper limit may indicate a developing fault even if it has not yet crossed the threshold.
Response rule:
Green band rising toward amber → reduce load, note in log.
Amber band → active monitoring every few minutes.
Amber to red boundary → controlled shut-down at next safe stop.
Red lamp illuminated → stop and shut down immediately.
Verify against model manual. Threshold values shown here are representative Gen6 ranges. Exact limits may differ for RK22, RK23, and RK24 — always confirm in the model-specific manual.
Operator action is based on trend and band, not a single gauge reading. Know the four thresholds for each system and act before a value reaches the red boundary.

Handoff Between Operators
Section 4
Operator Handoff Sequence
1
Outgoing Operator Brings Machine to Safe Hold
Complete the current work cycle. Bring unit to a designated handoff point — clear of loads, on level ground, park brake set, engine at low-idle.
OUTGOING
2
Outgoing Completes Shift Log Entry
Record hour meter reading, any fault codes observed, fluid top-ups performed, and anomalies noted during the shift. Entry must be complete before verbal handoff begins.
OUTGOING
3
Verbal Brief — Outgoing to Incoming
Cover: machine condition, any active or cleared faults, fuel and fluid status, any deferred items, and any site-specific hazards observed during the shift.
BOTH PRESENT
4
Incoming Operator Performs Walk-Around
Do not accept the machine on the verbal brief alone. Incoming operator completes an abbreviated pre-op check: fluids, visible damage, tyre/track condition, attachment security.
INCOMING
5
Incoming Signs On — Outgoing Signs Off
Both operators sign the shift log (or digital equivalent). Incoming's name and time recorded as accepting responsibility. Outgoing's shift is formally closed.
BOTH SIGN
What the Handoff Must Transfer
Machine Status
Hour meter reading, fuel level, hydraulic fluid level, any top-ups performed during shift
Fault History
All fault codes seen, even if cleared. Whether codes re-appeared. Any amber lamps that came and went.
Deferred Items
Any defect noted but not resolved — seal weep, unusual noise, slow travel — that incoming must monitor.
Site Conditions
Ground conditions, overhead hazards, traffic patterns, or environmental changes observed during the shift.
Work Order / Task Status
What work was completed, what is in-progress, what is pending — so the incoming can prioritise without rework.
Do Not Accept If…
Active red fault lamp. Raised unsecured load. Missing log entry. Outgoing unable to brief. Escalate to supervisor.
Accepting the machine = accepting responsibility. If you did not perform the check, you cannot sign the log.

Key Takeaways
Course Summary
Six sections, one machine. These are the critical concepts from each section that stay with you on the job.
1
Documentation, Safety and Support
Know Your Documents Before You Need Them
Three document tiers: G6 Reference Guide, model manuals, OEM vendor docs
Operator role is bounded — safety, warranty, and escalation rules are non-negotiable
Support forms exist for a reason — complete data creates a traceable service history
2
Platform Overview
RK22, RK23, RK24 Are Not the Same Machine
Core subsystems are shared — but specifications, limits, and fault codes differ by model
The RK24 has the IFM control system and C6 OEM vendor document package — unique to that model
Verify the model before opening any procedure or applying any threshold value
3
Pre-Operation and Startup
Three Gates — All Must Pass
Gate 1: pre-op walk-around complete. Gate 2: panel clear and neutral confirmed. Gate 3: warm-up done, all systems in range
Hold points cannot be bypassed — identify the cause before attempting any restart
Every startup requires a log entry — fault codes observed, even if cleared, must be recorded
4
Normal Operation and Monitoring
Monitor Trend, Not Just Snapshot
Four systems to track: coolant temp, oil pressure, hydraulic temp, air reservoir pressure
Respond before a red lamp — proactive load reduction prevents forced shutdowns
Operators control inputs; technicians adjust components — know the boundary
5
Shutdown and Service Interface
A Shift Closes With the Log Entry
Lower loads, cool down the turbo, park on level ground — the shutdown sequence is not optional
Post-op inspection is the best time to find faults — warm machine, defects are visible
The service handoff packet is the operator's final responsibility — incomplete packets delay repairs
6
Advanced Service Reference
Every Symptom Has a Document Path
31 OEM documents across 16 vendor families — know the subsystem first, then go to the bundle
The IFM CR0020 is the fault source — the CR1081 display is the window, not the origin
Documentation at every step: operator observation → technician diagnosis → service record closes the loop
The common thread across all six sections: Observe accurately. Document completely. Stay within your role. Escalate when in doubt. These four habits protect the equipment, the operator, and the organisation.

Common Adjustments in Operation
Section 4
Operator-Level Adjustments
⚙
Throttle / Engine Speed Control
Adjust engine speed to match work demand. High-idle for heavy lifting and travel; low-idle during standby. Do not run at high-idle unloaded for extended periods.
OPERATOR
🔄
Direction and Speed Selection
Use the directional selector and speed range to match grade and load. Downshift before steep grades — do not change direction under load without coming to a full stop first.
OPERATOR
💧
Hydraulic Flow Adjustments (Operator Control)
Modulate control inputs to reduce heat buildup during sustained actuation. If hydraulic temp rises above normal, pause and allow idle cool-down before resuming heavy actuation.
OPERATOR
🌡
Load Reduction in Response to Temps
If coolant or hydraulic temp approaches the upper band, reduce duty cycle before a warning lamp activates. Proactive load reduction prevents a forced shutdown.
OPERATOR
🔔
Horn and Light Controls
Use horn before moving and when entering blind areas. Activate work lights as required for site conditions — not for decoration. Leaving lights on at shutdown drains the battery.
OPERATOR
Operator vs. Service Boundary
These adjustments look similar but require different authorisation levels. Never cross the boundary without proper authorisation and documentation.
AdjustmentWho?Why the line is here
Engine throttle / speed selectorOPERATORControl input only — no hardware change involved.
Hydraulic relief valve pressure settingSERVICERequires calibrated gauge, procedure, and torque seal. Wrong setting causes system damage or safety risk.
Directional selector / gear rangeOPERATORNormal operational control — within manufacturer design.
Flow control valve setting on circuitSERVICEAffects cylinder speed; must match spec. Incorrect setting risks structural overload.
Idle speed dial (if fitted)OPERATOROperator-accessible control with defined travel range.
Engine governor or fuel rack adjustmentSERVICECertified technician only. Affects emissions and engine warranty.
Load shedding / duty cycle reductionOPERATORReducing work intensity is always within operator authority.
Air brake proportioning valveSERVICESafety-critical circuit. Requires post-adjustment brake test.
If in doubt, it's a service item. Operators control inputs — technicians adjust components.

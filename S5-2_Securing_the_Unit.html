Securing the Unit
Section 5
Controlled Shutdown Sequence
1
Complete the Current Work Cycle
Finish the load cycle in progress. Do not shut down mid-lift or mid-travel. Bring the machine to a logical stop point at the end of the task.
2
Lower All Attachments and Loads
Fully lower any raised bucket, forks, blade, or attachment to the ground. Do not park with any load raised — hydraulic pressure bleeds off during shutdown.
⚠ A load raised at shutdown will drop when hydraulic pressure is lost.
3
Park on Level Ground — Set Park Brake
Move to a designated parking area on firm, level ground where possible. Engage park brake. Select neutral or park on the transmission selector.
4
Reduce to Low Idle — Allow Cool-Down
Allow engine to idle at low speed for 3–5 minutes before shutdown. This allows turbocharger bearings and exhaust components to cool before oil flow stops.
⚠ Immediate shutdown from high load risks turbo bearing damage.
5
Shut Down Engine — Remove Key
Turn key to off. Wait for engine to fully stop. Remove key from ignition. Do not leave the key in an unattended machine.
6
Master Electrical Switch Off
Disengage master electrical switch to prevent battery drain during extended parking. Required on all overnight or multi-day park periods.
Confirmed Secure State
After shutdown, verify the unit has reached a fully secured state before leaving. These are the minimum confirmed-secure conditions.
Attachment Position
Lowered to ground
All hydraulic attachments fully down. No suspended loads. Blade or bucket flat on surface.
Park Brake
Engaged and confirmed
Park brake lamp confirmed on. On a grade, also chock wheels or tracks as a secondary measure.
Transmission
Neutral or Park
Selector confirmed in neutral or park position. Not left in a forward or reverse direction.
Engine
Off — fully stopped
Engine RPM zero, all gauges dropped. Not left idling unattended for any reason.
Ignition Key
Removed from machine
Key on operator's person or secured in key cabinet — not left in the cab or on the seat.
Master Electrical
Off (extended park)
Required for overnight or multi-day parking. Not required for brief mid-shift stops.
A machine is not secured until all six conditions are met. Partially secured machines are a site safety and liability exposure.

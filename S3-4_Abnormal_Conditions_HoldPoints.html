Abnormal Conditions and Hold Points
Section 3
A
Amber Warning Lamp During Self-Test Holdover
An amber lamp that remains on after the self-test cycle indicates a non-critical fault. Record the fault code, consult model manual. Do not proceed until identified.
HOLD
identify fault
B
Red Warning Lamp During Self-Test Holdover
A red lamp remaining after self-test signals a critical system fault. Do not start the engine. Secure the machine and escalate to service immediately.
NO START
escalate now
C
Oil Pressure Fails to Rise After Crank
If oil pressure does not enter the operating band within 10 seconds of engine fire, shut down immediately. Do not attempt a second start without identifying the cause.
SHUT DOWN
check oil / leaks
D
Abnormal Noise or Vibration on Startup
Any unusual knock, squeal, or excessive vibration at startup is a hold condition. Shut down and inspect before proceeding. Do not assume the condition will clear under load.
SHUT DOWN
inspect / report
E
Air Pressure Cannot Build to Operating Level
If reservoirs do not reach operating pressure within the expected build time, the brake circuit is not safe to release. Park brake must remain set. Escalate — do not move the machine.
LOCKED
brakes held
Hold-Point Rule
A hold point is a mandatory stop in the startup sequence. No hold point may be bypassed. If the condition causing the hold cannot be resolved by the operator, the machine must be locked out and service notified.
Fault Code Documentation
Write down the exact fault code displayed — do not rely on memory. The code number determines whether the fault is operator-resolvable or service-level. Fault codes are model-specific; always use the correct model manual for lookup.
Never Override a Red Lamp
Resetting the panel to clear a red fault lamp does not fix the fault. The condition will reappear — often at a worse stage. Clearing without diagnosis voids the service record and may void warranty coverage.
Operator boundary: Amber lamps — identify and document. Red lamps, pressure failures, abnormal noise — stop, secure, escalate. Operators do not diagnose beyond their role.

Startup Confirmation and Recordkeeping
Section 3
Final Startup Confirmation Checklist
Complete this gate before releasing the park brake or engaging any work circuit.
All Warning Lamps Out
No amber or red lamps illuminated after warm-up. If any remain, resolve before proceeding.
Engine Temp in Operating Band
Coolant temperature gauge reading within normal range. Warm-up not cut short.
Air Reservoirs at Full Pressure
Primary and secondary circuits at or above minimum operating pressure. Brakes safe to release.
Hydraulic Oil Temp in Range
Hydraulic system at minimum operating temperature before actuating any cylinders or motors.
Pre-Op Log Entry Made
Date, unit ID, operator name, meter reading, and any noted anomalies entered in the shift log.
Fault Codes Recorded (if any)
Any fault codes observed during startup written down — not cleared — before the shift begins.
Unchecked boxes = hold point. A shift does not begin until all boxes are checked or the hold is escalated.
Required Log Entries — Data Quality
Every startup requires a log entry. Incomplete or illegible entries degrade the service history and may invalidate warranty claims.
Unit ID / Serial Number
Date
Operator Name
Hour Meter Reading
Fault Codes Observed (if none, write "none")
Shift Start Time
Anomalies or Observations (fluids, noise, visual)
✓ Good Entry
Unit RK24-0042 · 27 May · J. Smith · 1,847 hrs · Fault: none · Slight hydraulic drip at left cylinder seal — noted for service
✗ Poor Entry
"Fine" — no unit ID, no meter reading, no fault note, no operator name. Cannot support warranty or service review.
Tip: If "none" is noted for faults, that entry is as important as a fault code — it establishes a baseline that the machine was clean at that point in time.
